# </u> Bio </u> Dakota Roberson <br /> Asst. Professor <br /> Electrical & Computer Engineering <br /> University of Idaho<br /> ## </u> Education </u> </u> Ph.D., Electrical Engineering </u> <br /> University of Wyoming <br /> *Graduate Minor Statistics* <br /> *On Loop-Shaped HVDC Wide-Area Damping Control* <br /> </u> B.S., Electrical Engineering (Hons.) </u> <br /> University of Wyoming <br /> *Minor Mathematics* <br /> *Cum Laude* <br /> ## </u> Short Biography </u> Dakota Roberson is a White House Fellow at the Department of Defense. Prior to this assignment, he was an Assistant Professor of Electrical and Computer Engineering at the University of Idaho and an Associate of the Center for Advanced Energy Studies. Dr. Roberson leads an interdisciplinary research team studying electrical grid stability and security and is an appointed Nuclear Engineering Affiliate Faculty. He has collaborated with numerous national laboratories, electric utilities, private stakeholders, and universities to mitigate 21st century energy system threats; his engineering courses are also structured to prepare students for careers in this area. Before joining the University of Idaho, he was with Sandia National Laboratories. To better serve his region, Dr. Roberson promotes STEM education through secondary school outreach and public speaking engagements to spark the curiosity of young scientists. He also volunteers as an advisor to regional energy infrastructure programs and a tech startup. Dakota was awarded the Ph.D. in Electrical Engineering with a Graduate Minor in Statistics at the University of Wyoming, where he won the Fisher Innovation Challenge for contributions to energy storage control. He holds a B.S. in Electrical Engineering, Minor Mathematics, from the same institution. In his free time, he races motocross and is an outdoorsman, and can be found playing guitar or painting during the winter months.
